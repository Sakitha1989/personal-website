Wind Speed Modeling & Forecasting

Advanced statistical simulation using change point detection for improved renewable energy forecasting

Research Contributions

Change point detection
Vector autoregression
Block bootstrap method

Challenge

Traditional wind speed forecasting methods fail to capture structural changes in wind patterns over time and across multiple locations. This leads to suboptimal energy production planning and increased operational costs for wind farms and grid operators.

Solution

Developed a novel nonparametric change point detection method for multivariate wind speed time series that:

  • Preserves spatio-temporal correlations across multiple wind measurement locations
  • Maintains covariance structure while detecting structural breaks
  • Enables more accurate scenario generation for renewable energy planning
  • Improves economic dispatch optimization in power systems

Technical Implementation

Core Technologies:
R Vector Autoregression (VAR) Block Bootstrap Change Point Detection Time Series Analysis
Key Components:
  • Multivariate Change Point Detection: Custom algorithm to identify structural breaks across multiple wind measurement stations simultaneously
  • VAR Model Framework: Vector autoregression models to capture cross-correlations between different wind measurement locations
  • Block Bootstrap Method: Preserves temporal dependencies while generating realistic wind speed scenarios
  • Economic Dispatch Integration: Interface with power system optimization models for real-world application
Industry Applications
Wind Farm Operators
  • Improved energy production forecasting accuracy
  • Better maintenance scheduling based on wind patterns
  • Enhanced operational planning capabilities
Grid Operators & Energy Traders
  • Enhanced renewable integration planning
  • Improved grid stability and load balancing
  • Better risk assessment for wind-based portfolios

Results & Validation

Validated using real wind farm data, demonstrating improvements over existing methods:

  • Tested on multiple years of hourly wind speed data from multiple measurement stations
  • Outperformed traditional ARIMA and persistence models
  • Successfully integrated with economic dispatch optimization models
  • Published in Applied Energy (high-impact peer-reviewed journal)
